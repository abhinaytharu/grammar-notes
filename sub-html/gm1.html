✅ 1. "Will"

Use it when:

  • a) Deciding at the moment of speaking (instant decisions):
    “I’m thirsty.”
    “I will get you some water.”
  • b) Offers, promises, threats:
    “I will help you with your homework.”
    “I will never forget you.”
  • c) Predictions based on opinions or beliefs (not evidence):
    “I think it will rain tomorrow.”

✅ 2. "Be going to"

Use it when:

  • a) Plans or intentions (already decided):
    “I’m going to start studying after dinner.”
  • b) Predictions based on present evidence:
    “Look at those clouds! It’s going to rain.”

🔁 Comparison Example:

  • “I will call her tonight.”
    → You just decided this now.
  • “I’m going to call her tonight.”
    → You already planned this earlier.
Situation Use
Instant decision will
Prior plan/intention going to
